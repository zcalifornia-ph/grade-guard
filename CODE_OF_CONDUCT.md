# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Grade Guard is intended to be a respectful, student-friendly, and technically serious project space.
Contributors, maintainers, and participants are expected to act in good faith and help keep collaboration constructive, inclusive, and professionally grounded.

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate clearly, respectfully, and with technical honesty.
- Critique ideas, code, and outcomes without attacking people.
- Credit the work of others and avoid plagiarism or fabricated claims.
- Share context, evidence, and reproducible steps when reporting issues.
- Be mindful that the project concerns academic progress and student data.
- Help make the repository welcoming to contributors with different backgrounds and experience levels.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, hate speech, discrimination, or demeaning conduct.
- Threats, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Doxxing or sharing private information without explicit consent.
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Plagiarism, misrepresentation, falsified test results, or other deceptive conduct.
- Retaliation against anyone who raises a good-faith concern.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, code review, discussions, documentation, and any public or private communication reasonably tied to Grade Guard project work.

## Reporting

Report concerns to `zecalifornia@up.edu.ph`.
Include relevant links, screenshots, logs, or other context when possible.
Reports made in good faith will be handled as confidentially as practical.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may moderate content, request behavior changes, remove contributions, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants when necessary to protect the project and its community.
Enforcement decisions should be proportionate to the behavior and guided by community safety, fairness, and repository health.
